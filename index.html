üå± What is Regenerative Agriculture?

Regenerative agriculture actively improves soil health, biodiversity, and ecosystems. Unlike conventional farming that depletes soil, regenerative practices build soil carbon, increase water retention, and can remove CO‚ÇÇ from the atmosphere.

üöú Conventional vs. Regenerative

Conventional farming uses synthetic fertilizers and monocultures for high short-term yields but degrades soil and releases CO‚ÇÇ. Regenerative practices like cover cropping and no-till farming build living soil that needs fewer inputs and sequesters carbon.

üí° Key Practices

Cover Crops: Plants grown between crops to protect and enrich soil.
Crop Rotation: Changing crops each season breaks pest cycles.
No-Till: Avoiding plowing keeps carbon stored underground.
Composting: Returns organic matter to feed soil microbes.
